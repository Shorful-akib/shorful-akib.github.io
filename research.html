Network-Adjusted Empirical Bayes Estimation of Age-Specific Adult Mortality from DHS Sibling Survival Histories: Evidence from Nigeria

Motivation: Adult mortality is difficult to measure directly in countries with incomplete vital registration. DHS sibling survival histories offer indirect evidence, but estimates are noisy at fine age and regional resolution.

Methodology: Empirical Bayes estimation with a network-adjustment step that borrows strength across related age groups and regions, improving stability of age-specific mortality estimates from sparse survey data.

Data: Nigeria Demographic and Health Survey (DHS) sibling survival histories.

Status: Manuscript complete; submission/preprint in progress. First and corresponding author.

Decomposing Urban–Rural Inequalities in Maternal Health Care Utilization in Bangladesh

Motivation: Maternal health service utilization differs sharply between urban and rural Bangladesh; understanding the drivers of this gap informs targeted policy.

Methodology: Decomposition analysis applied to nationally representative health survey data.

Status: Published, Heliyon.

Health Literacy and Associated Factors Among University Students in Bangladesh

Motivation: Health literacy shapes health-seeking behavior; university students are an understudied population in Bangladesh.

Methodology: Survey design, data collection, and statistical analysis of associated factors, supported by a systematic review of existing literature.

Status: Forthcoming, BMC Public Health (co-first author); related systematic review under review.
