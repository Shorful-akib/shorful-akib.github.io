Research Interests

Areas of Focus

Core methodological interests and how they connect to my applied work.

Bayesian Statistics

Probabilistic modeling that formally incorporates prior knowledge and quantifies uncertainty — the foundation of my methodological approach across projects.

Empirical Bayes

Estimating prior distributions directly from data to stabilize noisy, sparse estimates — the core technique in my DHS mortality estimation work.

Survival Analysis

Modeling time-to-event data, including age-specific mortality, under incomplete or indirect observation.

Biostatistics & Health Statistics

Statistical methods applied to health outcomes, health service utilization, and population health disparities.

Demography

Quantitative methods for estimating mortality, fertility, and population dynamics, particularly in data-sparse settings.

Machine Learning for Health Data

Supervised and unsupervised methods (ensembles, clustering, dimensionality reduction) applied to health and survey data, complementing classical statistical approaches.

Open to PhD research opportunities in Bayesian statistics, biostatistics, and demographic/health data science.
